From Doorstep to Dystrophin

A Patient Advocacy & Operations Strategy for Entrada Therapeutics

Executive Summary

The Challenge

Entrada's DMD trials face critical barriers: immense logistical burdens on families, caregiver fatigue, and a lack of diversity in recruitment. These issues lead to high dropout rates and limit the generalizability of trial data.

The Solution

A patient-first, three-pillar strategy focusing on Family-First Logistics, Story-Driven Education, and Equity by Design will dismantle these barriers, improve recruitment and retention, and set a new standard for patient advocacy in rare disease.

The Duchenne Landscape: A Foundation for Action

To design effective trials, we must anchor our strategy in the reality of the families we serve. DMD is a severe genetic disorder where the barriers to trial participation are painfully human:

Logistical & Financial Burden

Extensive travel and demanding procedures prevent nearly two-thirds of eligible families from participating in rare-disease trials.

Psychosocial & Emotional Toll

The dual challenge of securing a child's assent and managing caregiver exhaustion creates a significant retention risk.

Diversity & Equity Gaps

Black and Latino boys are often missed by traditional recruitment, fostering mistrust and yielding non-generalizable data.
