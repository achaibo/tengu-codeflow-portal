Case Study: Strategic Management of a Complex Global NSCLC Trial

Navigating Companion Diagnostics, Global Startup Hurdles, and Health Equity Integration with Masterful Precision.

Key Challenges

This global NSCLC trial involved a PD-L1 companion diagnostic and exploratory biomarkers (EGFR, ROS1, TROP2), presenting a confluence of critical operational hurdles:

  • ➤Regulatory Delays: Significant EUCTR and IVDR approval hurdles, especially for qualifying the PD-L1 testing lab in Scotland.
  • ➤Startup Bottlenecks: Complex startup processes across Europe and South America requiring dynamic site selection and tiering.
  • ➤Logistical Gaps: Inconsistent lab capabilities for exploratory biomarkers necessitating central lab support.
  • ➤Recruitment & DEI: Widespread recruitment difficulties coupled with the mandate for robust DEI integration in North America.
  • ➤Data Integrity: Immense pressure to ensure data quality for an upcoming futility analysis amid competing regional timelines.

1. Dynamic Study Startup in Europe

Aggressively mitigated EUCTR & IVDR delays through a hands-on, data-driven strategy.

Week-by-Week Tracking Site Tiering RFI Management Country Lead Coordination

2. DEI & Decentralized Trials (DCT)

Embedded equity into the trial's fabric and reduced patient burden in North America.

Community Partnerships Inclusive Recruitment Telemedicine Remote Monitoring

3. Cross-Regional Enrollment

Managed competing timelines between rapidly enrolling APAC and slower EU/American regions.

Region-Specific Targets Dynamic Re-forecasting Agile Strategy

4. Biomarker & Lab Management

Ensured testing integrity for the critical PD-L1 CDx and exploratory biomarkers.

Central Lab Oversight IVDR Compliance Dedicated Workflow Team

5. PI Engagement & Recruitment

Re-energized disengaged sites and filled recruitment gaps across Europe and South America.

MSL Collaboration Performance Dashboards Targeted Outreach

6. Monitoring & Data Integrity

Maintained data quality under pressure for the critical futility analysis decision point.

Real-Time Dashboards Futility Analysis Readiness Data Management Coordination

Conclusion

This trial demonstrated the operational complexity of managing a global NSCLC program with a PD-L1 companion diagnostic under EUCTR and IVDR regulations. By implementing a dynamic study startup model, maintaining tight lab oversight, integrating DEI strategies, and leveraging decentralized trial tools, we ensured regulatory compliance, improved patient access, and maintained momentum despite multi-regional startup delays.
