Patient Advocacy Strategy for Prime Medicine’s Genetic Therapies

By Tengu Muna | Global Clinical Consultant / Patient Advocacy

💡 "In the race for a cure, brilliant science provides the engine, but strategic execution is the accelerator. My focus is on empowering patients and building the operational bridge that carries groundbreaking therapies from the lab to the lives they are meant to transform."

I. Introduction: Prime Medicine’s Patient-First Vision 🎯

Prime Medicine is pioneering Prime Editing, a next-generation gene-editing technology designed to “search and replace” faulty DNA segments with precision, minimal off-target effects, and broad applicability across nearly 90% of known disease-causing mutations.

Their Patient-First Strategy is more than a mission statement — it’s a mandate to ensure every clinical, regulatory, and operational decision reflects the voice, needs, and lived experiences of patients and caregivers. This strategy is particularly vital for rare diseases.

Prioritizing accessibility and inclusion. Ensuring clear communication. Partnering early with advocacy groups. Embedding DEI into recruitment.

II. Pipeline-Aligned Patient Advocacy Strategies 🧬

1. Chronic Granulomatous Disease (CGD) 🦠

Definition: A rare immune disorder where white blood cells can't kill bacteria and fungi, leading to life-threatening infections.

Patient Vignette – Layla’s Story (Middle East): "Layla, 6, has spent more days in the hospital than in school. When offered a trial, her family’s main fear was travel. A localized support hub allowed her to participate without leaving her country, ensuring both treatment access and cultural familiarity."

Strategic Actions:

  • 🤝 Establish local trial access points in high-prevalence regions (Middle East/APAC).
  • 🤝 Create culturally adapted educational materials in Arabic, Farsi, Hindi, and English.
  • 🤝 Partner with regional immunology networks to pre-screen patients.
  • 🤝 Facilitate parent support circles to address fears and logistics.

2. Wilson’s Disease 🧠

Definition: A rare genetic disorder causing copper accumulation in the liver and brain. Untreated, it can lead to liver failure and neurological decline.

Patient Vignette – Marco’s Story (Italy): "Marco, a 19-year-old student, faced long delays for a diagnosis. A trial outreach campaign partnered with local clinics, helping patients like Marco get fast-tracked to genetic testing and enrollment without losing precious time."

Strategic Actions:

  • 🤝 Develop coordinated referral pathways with hepatology clinics.
  • 🤝 Create web-based patient navigators to guide families through trial eligibility.
  • 🤝 Launch localized outreach in Italy, China, and Japan with explainer videos.
  • 🤝 Partner with liver disease NGOs to integrate trial info into patient resource packets.

3. Cystic Fibrosis (CF) 🫁

Definition: A life-shortening, inherited disease affecting the lungs and digestive system due to mutations in the CFTR gene.

Patient Vignette – Jasmine’s Story (U.S.): "Jasmine, 15, juggles school and daily airway clearance. When invited to a trial, her family was reassured by remote monitoring and at-home nurse visits, which meant fewer school absences and better adherence."

Strategic Actions:

  • 🤝 Implement hybrid trial models combining home visits with in-clinic assessments.
  • 🤝 Engage youth-focused patient ambassadors to share experiences via social media.
  • 🤝 Provide nutrition and exercise support packages for holistic care.
  • 🤝 Launch advocacy-driven recruitment campaigns with CF Foundation chapters.

IV. Cross-Functional & Regional Integration 🌍

Across all programs, we will collaborate with global clinical operations to tailor patient engagement, embed advocacy input early in protocol design, and use decentralized tools to improve accessibility.

V. Conclusion & Next Steps 🚀

By aligning the patient-first philosophy with disease-specific, regionally nuanced advocacy strategies, Prime Medicine can improve recruitment, strengthen trust, and accelerate timelines from diagnosis to trial participation.
